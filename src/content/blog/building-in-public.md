Though shipping a portfolio is easy, building one that actually reflects who you are is the harder thing, and it is the one I wanted to do.

Most portfolios are graveyards: a list of projects you finished years ago, sitting untouched while the real work happens elsewhere. That is not what I was after. I wanted a site that grows as I do, reflects how I think, and actually brings value to the people who land on it.

### The constraints I gave myself

**No templates.** If someone can deploy the same site in five minutes, it is not a personal brand; it is a theme.

**Content first, design second.** A polished empty site is still empty, so the writing comes before the polish.

**Ship ugly, then refine.** The worst outcome is a perfect site that never launches, so I would rather launch rough and sharpen it in the open.

### What you'll find here

Projects I have built across consulting, product, and open source. Writing on business strategy, technology, and the overlap between the two. Honest post-mortems on the things that did not work.

If any of it is useful to you, even one idea, one framework, one honest mistake, then it is worth publishing.
